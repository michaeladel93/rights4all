Sports law

Duration: 30 hrs.
Certification: Rights For All Law Firm
Entry criteria: For all lawyers, law students and sportsmen.
Course language: Arabic
Target group: Law students and lawyers

Our course overview and objectives: Sports law became a very important area of practice today for many lawyers as its issues became trendy these days because of the disputes that arose between players, agents, associations and clubs related to contracts or obligations and duties. We aim to qualify law students, lawyers and any person concerned by sports to enter this field confidently and professionally. Our material contents: - Sources of Sport Law. - Defining the Olympic Charter beside the rights and duties of players towards the club. - The rights and duties of the players towards the players’ agents. - Drafting of professional sports and agents contracts. - The relation between the Football Association and the professional players. - Situations where players resort to the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS). -The penalties imposed by the CAS on players, clubs and agents. - The fault that violates the rules of sport practicing and its civil and criminal liability. - Comparative sports legislation between the Egyptian regime and the Gulf regimes (Qatar - Kuwait - Saudi Arabia). - The procedures of litigation submitted in arbitration and mediation in the light of Law No. 27-1994. - Mediation and arbitration sports center in the light of Law No. 72/2017. - The basic system of the Mediation and Arbitration sports center. - The role and jurisdiction of the CAS.

International Legal English

Duration: 36 hrs per level
Certification: Rights For All Law Firm
Entry criteria: To pass our placement test
Course language: English
Target group: Law students and other interested people

Our course overview and objectives: The International Legal English course is intended for individuals intending to use English for a law study or within a professional legal context. Persons needing to acquire a basic knowledge of legal English for interpreting or translating are also welcome. The course aims to improve the English language skills within a legal context. Participants learn to read and understand legal texts such as articles and legal correspondence. Also, they will learn to write letters and memos. Listening skills will be practiced by means of a variety of listening exercises, such as lectures, presentations and interviews. Speaking skills will be practiced through (group) discussions, presentations, interviews and role plays. The legal terminology of this course focuses on commercial law, civil law, property law and contract law. However, other fields of law, such as criminal law and comparative law will also be discussed. Our material contents: - Improving your ability to write common legal text types in English, such as letters or memoranda. - Improving your ability to read and understand legal texts, such as legal periodicals, commercial legislation, legal correspondences and other commercial law documents. - Increasing your comprehension of spoken English when it is used to speak about legal topics in meetings, presentations, interviews, discussions, etc. - Strengthening your speaking skills and enabling you to engage more effectively in a range of typical speaking situations of the legal practice.
